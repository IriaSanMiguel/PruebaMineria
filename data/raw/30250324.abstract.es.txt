In all cases, there was fever and purpuric rash; around 70% manifested myalgias and arthralgias; 28% presented digestive tract bleeding and 11% had severe neurological alterations. 64.2% of the cases received adequate treatment with doxycycline. Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever (RMSF) is caused by Rickettsia rickettsii. In Coahuila, Comarca Lagunera is considered an endemic zone; no cases have been reported in the southern zone of the state, specifically in the city of ­Saltillo.
The southern zone of Coahuila should be considered an endemic area for FMMR. The delay in diagnosis and treatment favor a greater lethality.
Prospective study, cases report. Children evaluated in the Hospital del Niño Dr. Federico Gómez Santos from September 2012 to September 2017, with clinically and laboratory (by polymerase chain reaction, PCR) confirmed diagnosis of FMMR were included. The epidemiological antecedents, clinical characteristics and patient’s evolution are presented.
